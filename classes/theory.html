IB MUSIC / MUSIC THEORY

The Music Theory course has four levels, each with honors.

The purpose of this class is to explore various styles of musical composition. It is a rigorous course with a very structured and regimented method. Students registered for class are expected to read music, both treble and bass clefs. A working knowledge of rhythm will be helpful, but not necessary. The main composition style that will be explored is that which was used by composers during the Common Practice Period (1500-1913). Composition will constitute the bulk of the course work and homework will be given.

Rules:

Students will be polite at all times and show respect for the classroom, the music, the teacher and each other.
Upon entering the classroom, students will prepare for class by assembling instruments/taking out notebook & homework. All students will view the board for a “warm up” or “do-now” activity.
The teacher may assign seats and may change the assigned seats at any time for any reason.
Students must report to class on time.
Outside of the designated lunch block, no food, gum, or drinks are allowed in the music room.
No touching the music stands/equipment without permission.
If you need to leave the room for any reason, you must sign out in the book. No student may leave the room during the first 15 minutes of the class unless it's an emergency.
Only one person is allowed to leave the class at a time.
Raise your hand when you have a question.
When the instructor is providing the lesson, students must remain quiet.
Students will refrain from doing work from other classes while in music theory class.
Students will take notes when appropriate.
Students will remain up-to-date on google classroom assignments.

Grading

Tests are worth 40% of the grade. They will be assigned each marking period and are based on the realization of SATB part-writing. If the test is given on paper, students may use their notebooks.
Quizzes and Compositions are worth 30% of the grade. At the end of each unit, students will be assigned a musical composition task to be completed and assessed. Students are encouraged to ask questions to classmates and to the teacher.
Homework is worth 30% of the grade. Part of this homework is to bring a notebook of staff paper each class. Homework is assigned per class and may be started in the last few minutes of class.
