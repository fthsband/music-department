Freehold Township Music Department

BAND BANQUET

The Music Parents will present an annual Band Awards Banquet each spring. All students are encouraged to attend. At the banquet, the below awards will be bestowed (mostly to seniors). Dress is semi-formal!

Each student will receive an annual participation award according to how many years he/she has completed in this band. Each student will also be given a certificate for every ensemble they were involved in.

First Year Award - Certificate of Participation
Second Year Award - Varsity Letter
Third Year Award - Recognition Pin (for varsity jacket and graduation stole)
Fourth Year Award - Recognition Plaque

Pit Orchestra
Flute Choir
Percussion Ensemble
Wind Ensemble
All Shore Jazz Band
All Shore Symphonic Band
Region II Wind Ensemble
Region II Symphonic Band
Region II Percussion Ensemble
All State Band

Along with the yearly awards, students can also be recognized for accomplishments like perfect attendance or being in honors band for a prolonged time. The full list is as follows:

-- Perfect Attendance Awards

Attended all rehearsals, games, parades, and concerts.

-- Instrumental Doubling and Tripling (and Beyond) Awards

Proficient in more than one instrument.

-- Most Improved Awards

Had the largest trend of improvement since the beginning of the year.

-- Came Out of Nowhere Awards

Had the largest trend of improvement since joining the band.

-- National School Marching Award

The National School Marching Award recognizes outstanding contributions on the marching field. It honors the musical contributions and dedication of student leaders whose example inspires other students.

-- Quincy Jones Award

In his long musical career Quincy Jones has done it all as a composer, arranger, performer, producer, and teacher. Every music program has those students who share this boundless energy and musical versatility. With his approval we initiate this award to encourage students in their pursuit of music.

-- First Chair Award

Presented to students for outstanding individual accomplishment(s) and commitment to the Music Program.

-- William J. Le Potomane Memorial Scholarship

Created to honor the service of Matt ('14), trumpet section leader, and Abby ('19), trombone player and student executive, Basen. Both will state that they were forced to participate in a band, most likely so their father could one day assume the presidency of the Music Parents. What they won't say is just how enjoyable the experience was for them both. Both Matt and Abby benefited from being part of the Pride of Freehold Township and speak fondly of their experiences & the lessons they learned from Mr. Gattsek & Mr. Crawford: the importance of teamwork, the benefits of taking on leadership roles, and the beauty of creating art. This scholarship was created to serve as a reminder of the many great years that the Basen Family had being part of the Freehold Township Marching Patriots family, the pride of Freehold Township.

-- Marvin Cooperman Memorial Award

This award is given in memory of a great friend of the FTHS Band program, Marvin Cooperman. Mr. Cooperman was an accomplished clarinet player and was a retired member of the 63rd Army Band and the 50th Armored Division Band. Originally from Newark, he spent his career as a shipping clerk for a major chemical company here in NJ, but like so many, music is what made his soul flourish. Marvin was a local Freehold Borough resident who for almost 20 years performed in and with our concert band and wind ensemble. His multi-generational influence and musical talents were a welcome addition to our band program. Marvin always told me that the quality of students and program we run here kept him feeling youthful even as he turned 70 and beyond. He was one of the funniest and most self-deprecating men I have ever met. He passed away in 2019 after a long bout with illness. May this award serve to remind us all of how important music is, no matter what career path you choose. RIP Marvin, we miss you.

-- National School Color Guard Award

The National School Color Guard Award recognizes outstanding contributions by color guard members.

-- United States Marine Corps Semper Fidelis Award

The “Semper Fidelis” Award for Musical Excellence is in recognition of diligence, dedication, and musical excellence as a performing high school bandsman.

-- First Presbyterian Church of Freehold Scholarship

Awarded in recognition of leadership to the Freehold Youth Ensemble and devotion to providing music opportunities for high school students.

-- Harrison and Matthew Shernicoff Esprit de Corps Scholarship

This scholarship, named to honor Harrison ('13), two-year trombone section leader, and Matthew ('17), trombone section leader and drum major, Shernicoff, will be awarded to a student who has achieved academic and instrumental success, and through their own actions and conduct, has promoted an esprit de corps (enthusiasm, devotion, and honor) as a member of the FTHS Instrumental Program.

-- Performing Arts Award

For Exceptional Achievements in Band, Choir and Drama and for Contributions to the Artistic Community of FTHS.

-- Emily Karr Service Award

This is a scholarship to honor the service of Emily Karr. Emily provided more conscientious service than any student in the history of FTHS. From recruitment, leadership, mentorship, and musicianship, she provided one of the best examples of behavior and contributions the band has ever known.

-- Louis Armstrong Jazz Award

In recognition of outstanding achievement by an instrumentalist in the field of jazz, as demonstrated through superior musicianship, character, and individual creativity.

-- Zieber R. Stetler Memorial Scholarship

Awarded in memory of Zieber R. Stetler, first band director of Freehold Township High School.

-- Leonard Bernstein Award

In his long career, Bernstein made an indelible mark on the musical world as a conductor, composer, and teacher. This award will honor students whose musical contributions, dedication, and creativity set them apart as deserving of the highest recognition for their achievements.

-- Annie Chernow Scholarship

A scholarship given to honor the service of Annie Chernow. Annie is a graduate of Freehold Twp HS, the University of Delaware and University of Minnesota Duluth. All of her degrees are in percussion. While in high school, Annie performed with every ensemble FTHS offered. She excelled in honors band and percussion ensemble participation. She was a member of the Region II Bands and the All Shore Bands. Throughout college, Annie marched multiple years with The Cadets Drum and Bugle Corps and has performed with the Duluth Symphony.

-- John Philip Sousa Award

In recognition of outstanding achievement and interest in instrumental music, for singular merit in instrumental music, for singular merit in loyalty and cooperation, and for displaying those high qualities of conduct that school instrumental music requires.

-- FTHS Music Parents Association Instrumental Music Scholarship

Presented to outstanding instrumental music students who plan to further their music education.

-- FTHS Music Parents Association President's Scholarship: Patriot Scholar in Music Award

Presented to a student who has demonstrated commitment and excellence to the performing arts.
