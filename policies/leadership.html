LEADERSHIP TEAM

Click here for the leadership packet.

The leadership team consists of students who are placed in charge of delegated tasks. The leadership team consists of the drum major, student executive, guard & section leaders, and morale officer. Auditions for the leadership team take place in May for the following year. Students with strong instrumental/performance skills and a desire to learn more about music and leadership are encouraged to inquire with the director.

Specific Roles

Drum Major


This student should be a junior or senior and be selected through a rigorous audition process. A panel of judges interviews and observes candidates conduct, march, call commands, and perform an original salute. An essay is required. All candidates will demonstrate memorization of all major scales. Only students who are registered in the band class for credit may serve as drum majors and have the right to vote in the student balloting for drum majors. The drum major should be of the utmost moral character, a top-notch student, and a role model. The drum major must consistently demonstrate strong leadership and motivational qualities and have knowledge of conducting music, and teaching drill and marching fundamentals. He/she is placed in charge of the band at all marching band rehearsals and performances. He/she is responsible only to the director. Talent, Ability, Appearance, and Confidence are important.



Student Executive

This is a student and upperclassman with tremendous organizational and people skills and is appointed by the director as a member of the leadership team. They are responsible for all logistical aspects of the band program. Attendance, music filing, photocopying, ability to act as treasurer, bus loading, uniform inventory, stage set up, strong computer skills, and effective communication skills are all integral to this position. The student executive is answerable to the drum major and the director. The student executive will nominate student delegates for logistical committees.



Section Leader, Pit Captain, and Drum Captain

Section leaders are upperclassmen who have demonstrated competence/mastery of their instrument and marching techniques. These people assist in the teaching and rehearsing of the marching band show. They must have earned a level of respect from their fellow bandsmen and have demonstrated leadership ability and desire. They must have the ability to lead their section while maintaining a positive rapport. They work under the supervision of the director and check in daily to see that they are well-prepared for each rehearsal. They are responsible for running music rehearsals when needed, checking tuning, and inspecting uniforms. Auditions require marching, memorization of all major scales, calling of commands, and composing an essay.



Color Guard Captain

The Guard Captain is a special type of section leader. The guard captain must exhibit strong leadership qualities, demonstrate extensive knowledge of marching fundamentals, equipment handling on rifle and flag, and possess advanced communication skills. A background in dance/choreography is helpful. Auditions require marching, original choreography, composing an essay, teaching a simple topic to someone who is not in the color guard, and calling commands.



Morale Officer

The morale officer is an upperclassman that is committed to generating goodwill and positive motivation within the band. This person must demonstrate a dynamic personality and an ability to remain up-beat and positive in the face of opposition. Student must be a role model at all times. An audition for the leadership team is required for consideration of this position.



Field Captain

This a senior post of a logistical nature. The Field Captain's job is to make sure that the field is ready for performance/rehearsal. From the podium, to the yard markers to having enough water, the field captains make the band happen! Student must be a role model at all times. An audition for the leadership team is required for consideration of this position.



Choreographer

This student is responsible for designing any dancing required in our shows. Student must have a dance background and be comfortable working with large groups. Furthermore, the choreographer must plan and be prepared to teach simple and complicated moves to students with little to no dance experience. The position is appointed annually. Any student with extensive dance/choreography experience should make it known to the director.
